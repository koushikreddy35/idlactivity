MS Dhoni will be captaining the Chennai Super Kings for a landmark 200th time when he leads the team out against Rajasthan Royals in their third round of the 2021 VIVO Indian Premier League season at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai on Monday (April 19, IST 7.30 p.m. onwards). The talismanic skipper guided the side to a six-wicket win in the previous game against Punjab Kings in what was his 200th outing for the three-time champions.Medium-pacer Deepak Chahar set up the victory with a sensational new-ball spell, returning his IPL-best figures of 4/13 that left the opposition struggling at 26/5 at one stage. “Over the years, he (Chahar) has matured into a death bowler too, but he’s somebody who gets more purchase than other bowlers,” Dhoni said of the opening bowler who finished his four overs in a single spell, topping it with a wicket-maiden. “If you are looking to attack, why not finish his four overs at the start. (Dwayne) Bravo can bowl at the death. It’s not a secret,” Dhoni added. PBKS were restricted to 106/8 and the Super Kings won with 4.2 overs to spare for their first victory of the season. “I think I got the responsibility to bowl the first over and set the tone,” Chahar told the official broadcasters. “It’s a big responsibility for me in the last four years and Mahi bhai has shown faith in me,” added the 28-year-old who was declared Player of the match. Chahar bowled 18 dot balls to put pressure on the opposition. “You may not take wickets in T20s, so my plan is to bowl dots in Powerplay,” he said. Moeen Ali (46) fell short of a well-deserved fifty after he put on 66 for the second wicket with opener Faf du Plessis (36 n.o.) to set up the chase. “Moeen is a very good timer of the ball and plays authentic shots. We felt if Moeen bats up the order, we can make use of the resources that we got. We have to make the most of our resources,” Dhoni said. RR also have one win from two matches.

“He has been absolutely the heartbeat of CSK, there is no doubt about it,” Head coach Stephen Fleming said of the team’s all-time skipper who made his 200th appearance for the three-time Indian Premier League champions on Friday (April 16). “Whether it’s performances or guidance or leadership and you still run out of things to say about him,” Fleming told a virtual post-match news conference after the Chennai Super Kings beat Punjab Kings by six wickets in their second outing of the 2021 VIVO IPL at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai. “But as longevity is also something now that is to be appreciated and mentioned, but to play 200 games and to still have a desire to do well and perform well is a testament to his attitude firstly towards the game but also the franchise as well. I think the franchise has grown and MS has grown with it. So it’s a very good relationship and a great fit,” the Kiwi added. The Super Kings are one of the most consistent teams in the IPL, having qualified for the Playoffs in 10 of their 11 seasons and entered the final on eight occasions. “Makes me feel very good and it has been a very long journey,” Dhoni told the official broadcasters about his personal landmark after the Super Kings registered their first win of the season. Medium-pacer Deepak Chahar ripped out PBKS’s top-half with his IPL-best figures of 4/13 that left the opposition struggling at 26/5 at one stage. “Without doubt when Deepak gets a little bit of swing and a little bit of movement he is one of the finest around. So his ability to swing the ball is one, but his control and also his skillset is very high,” Fleming said. “So the key really is keeping him a little bit simple in his plans and just operating in and around the stumps because he is so effective when he has the ball swinging. Today is a great example of the skills he has,” the Head coach added. The Super Kings next meet Rajasthan Royals at the same venue on Monday (April 19).

Chennai Super Kings will look to log their first points of the season when they take on Punjab Kings in their second match of the 2021 VIVO Indian Premier League at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai on Friday (April 16, IST 7.30 p.m. onwards) following a five-day break after their opening game at the same venue. The three-time champions finished on the wrong side of the result against Delhi Capitals in their first outing after a fabulous fifty by Suresh Raina got them to a competitive 188/7 on being put in to bat. CSK lost both openers by the third over before Raina anchored the recovery with two crucial fifty-run partnerships and Sam Curran-Ravindra Jadeja combo powered the finish with a 51-run stand. “You have to look ahead,” skipper MS Dhoni said about his side rallying from 7/2. “Especially when you have dew in your mind and you are batting first, you want to get those extra 10-15 runs — that was normally in 8 p.m. start games,” he told the official broadcasters. “(Now the) 7.30 start means the opposition will have at least half an hour when there will be very little amount of dew on the field, which means it will not come on as nicely as it will in the second innings. So you have to score those 15-20 extra runs to make it even, and after that also you have to get early wickets to make a big mark on the game,” he added. CSK’s death overs specialist Dwayne Bravo bowled superbly for figures of 1/28 from four overs after being introduced in the 12th over with DC requiring 76 for victory and their openers going strong. The experienced all-rounder took out a rampaging Prithvi Shaw and put the brakes on the opposition with his guile before DC won with eight balls to spare. South Africa tearaway Lungi Ngidi won’t be available for the second match as he remains in quarantine, CSK Head coach Stephen Fleming said. “(Jason) Behrendorff (Josh Hazlewood’s replacement) would obviously be following that. It’s an area that we are probably short on. But we still look at the Indian bowlers and we have international bowler Sam Curran and probably expect a little bit more (from the pacers),” Fleming added. It will also be PBKS’ second outing after they made a winning start to the season.

In what was easily one of the most exciting last over in the history of the Indian Premier League, Mitchell Santner, playing only his second IPL game, hit a six off the final ball to help Chennai Super Kings beat Rajasthan Royals by four wickets for what was MS Dhoni’s 100th IPL win as captain. Dhoni the finisher took the chase down to the wire after helping the three-time champions recover from 24/4 by scoring a 43-ball 58 (2x4, 3x6) and share in a 95-run stand for the fifth wicket with Ambati Rayudu (57, 47b, 2x4, 3x6). With 18 required off the last over, bowled by Ben Stokes, Ravindra Jadeja blasted a six back over the bowler’s head even as the southpaw lost balance and fell over. A single off a no-ball and a two by Player of the match Dhoni off the resultant free hit saw the Super Kings inch closer to the target of 152, but the skipper was dismissed off the next delivery, which left the side needing eight off three balls when Santner walked in. The New Zealander took two runs off the fourth delivery which was a waist-high full toss that was signaled as a no-ball by the straight umpire but over-ruled by the square-leg umpire, and two more off the fifth ball. Stokes delivered a wide with the Super Kings needing four to win and Santner brought up the victory in style as he got into position early to a full-length delivery outside off-stump and lofted it high over long-on.
